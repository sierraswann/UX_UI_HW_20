Wander Woman

Year: 2020
Duration: 2 Weeks
Sierra Swann | Chelsea Robinson

The Problem:
 Women who travel are constantly worried about safety and expenses when they travel domestically and abroad.
They want to find a safe place to stay that is away from touristy areas so they can have a more authentic, local experience.
           
           
The Solution:
To make female traveling experiences more enjoyable, we intend to develop an app that will offer women
real-time emergency assistance. The app will also alert them to local, female-owned establishments and offer a chat room
for connections in country.
          
           
My Role:
UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer
          
           
Tools:
 Adobe XD, Google Suite, Miro, Zoom, Google Forms, Otter

Research

My partner and I started this project together and created a Proto-Persona named Penelope Roberts. Penelope is 28 years old and works as a Remote Film Editor. This allows her to fuel her love of travel.

From our research, we were able to obtain 23 survey results from women around the world and interviewed 5 women who travel frequently and have even lived as expats in foreign countries. We had 2 weeks to research, quantify, and design this app.

Tasked with finding a way to make traveling easier, I instantly thought of making an app for women. I was interested in knowing these 4 things about female travelers:


  • what experiences women had while traveling
  • what resources they used to find places to stay while traveling
  • what they like to do when they travel
  • what do they take into consideration when they are planning their trips

My partner and I performed a competitor analysis of a few different apps and websites. Overall, the idea seemed to be done before, but it was up to us to give our app its own unique flavor.
